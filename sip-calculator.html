What is a SIP?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is one of the most popular ways to invest in mutual funds in India. Instead of investing a large amount at once, you invest a fixed amount every month. This disciplined approach helps you benefit from rupee cost averaging — buying more units when markets are low and fewer when they're high — smoothing out market volatility over time.

With as little as ₹500 per month, you can start a SIP in most mutual funds in India through platforms like Groww, Zerodha Coin, or directly through the AMC's website.

SIP Formula

The future value of a SIP is calculated using this formula:

M = P × ({[1 + r]^n – 1} / r) × (1 + r)

Where: M = Maturity amount | P = Monthly SIP amount | r = Monthly rate of return (Annual return ÷ 12 ÷ 100) | n = Number of months

Important: Do not divide annual return by 12 directly. A 12% annual return = 0.9489% monthly (not 1%), because returns compound. Our calculator uses the correct formula.

What is a Step-Up SIP?

A Step-Up SIP allows you to increase your monthly investment amount by a fixed percentage every year. For example, if you start with ₹5,000/month and set a 10% annual step-up, your SIP becomes ₹5,500 in Year 2, ₹6,050 in Year 3, and so on. This mirrors salary growth and significantly boosts your final corpus.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 12% annual return realistic for SIP?
Historically, large-cap equity mutual funds in India have delivered 10–14% CAGR over long periods (10+ years). Flexi-cap and small-cap funds have delivered more, but with higher volatility. For planning purposes, 10–12% is a conservative assumption. Past returns don't guarantee future performance.
Is SIP better than a lump sum investment?
SIP is generally safer for most investors because it removes the need to time the market. If you have a large amount to invest and markets are at a low, lump sum can outperform SIP. For regular monthly savings (like from your salary), SIP is the ideal approach.
Can I stop or pause my SIP anytime?
Yes. Most mutual funds allow you to pause or cancel a SIP with a simple online request, usually with 7–10 days notice before the next instalment date. There is no exit penalty for stopping a SIP, though ELSS (tax-saving) SIPs have a 3-year lock-in per instalment.
What is XIRR and how is it different from SIP returns shown here?
XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) is the actual annualised return considering the timing of each investment. The returns in this calculator assume a constant rate, which is an approximation. For evaluating actual mutual fund performance, XIRR is the correct measure, and you can calculate it in Excel or any financial calculator.
How much should I invest in SIP each month?
A common guideline is to invest at least 20% of your monthly income in SIPs for long-term goals. For specific goals like retirement, children's education, or buying a home, use the goal-based approach: set the target amount, timeline, and expected return, then work backwards to find the required monthly SIP.
