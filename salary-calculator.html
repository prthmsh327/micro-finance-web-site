What is the Difference Between CTC and In-Hand Salary?

CTC (Cost to Company) is the total annual expense a company incurs for an employee. It includes your basic salary, HRA, allowances, employer's PF contribution, and sometimes gratuity provision. However, your actual take-home (in-hand) salary is significantly lower because several deductions happen:

Deductions from your salary: Employee PF (12% of basic, capped at ₹1,800/month for basic ≤ ₹15,000 or 12% of actual basic if higher), Professional Tax (state-dependent, typically ₹200/month), and Income Tax (TDS based on your annual taxable income and chosen tax regime).

This calculator gives you a simplified view without income tax deductions. For exact TDS, use our Tax Regime Comparator to find your annual tax liability, then divide by 12 to get monthly TDS.

How Is PF Calculated?

Employee PF contribution is 12% of your basic salary. However, if your basic salary exceeds ₹15,000/month, both you and your employer can cap contributions at 12% of ₹15,000 = ₹1,800/month each, unless your company follows the higher-salary PF rule.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my actual in-hand salary different from this calculator?
This calculator uses standard assumptions for salary structure. Your actual salary structure depends on your offer letter. Companies vary in how they split CTC into basic, HRA, LTA, food coupons, medical allowance, and special allowances. Check your salary slip for the exact breakdown.
What is Special Allowance and is it taxable?
Special Allowance is the residual amount in your salary after basic, HRA, and other defined allowances. It is fully taxable. Many companies put a large portion of salary under special allowance because it's flexible. In your salary slip, it's the "balancing" component.
Can I opt out of PF if my salary is high?
If you are joining a new organization and your salary is above ₹15,000/month (basic), you can opt out of PF by submitting Form 11. However, this must be done at the time of joining. Once enrolled, you cannot opt out during employment with the same employer.
Is HRA exemption available in the new tax regime?
No. HRA exemption is only available under the old tax regime. Under the new tax regime, HRA is fully taxable as part of your gross salary. This is a major factor to consider when choosing between old and new regime — check our Tax Regime Calculator for the full comparison.
